# Outreach Templates & Scripts

## EMAIL TEMPLATES

### Template 1: Discovery Email (Cold Outreach)
```
Subject: Quick question about [Business Name]'s website

Hi [Owner Name],

I was looking for [plumber/electrician/salon] in [City] and noticed 
you don't have a website yet. 

That's actually an opportunity — most of your customers are probably 
searching online right now and finding your competitors instead.

I specialize in getting local service businesses online quickly. Just 
built a site for [similar business in city], and they're already getting 
calls from it.

I can have a professional, mobile-friendly website up for you in 3 days 
for $500. No long contracts, no upsells.

Want to hop on a quick 15-minute call to see if it makes sense? 

Just reply with your availability and I'll send you a link.

[Your Name]
[Your Phone]
[Your Email]
```

**When to send:** Monday-Thursday, 9am-12pm (business hours)
**Follow-up:** If no response in 5 days, send follow-up #1

---

### Template 2: Follow-Up #1 (After 5 days)
```
Subject: Re: Quick question about [Business Name]'s website

[Owner Name],

Didn't hear back from my last email — just wanted to circle back.

Thought you might find this interesting: I checked Google Maps for 
"[industry] in [city]" and you're getting buried by competitors with 
websites. 

If you're interested in fixing that (and it's only $500), let me know.

If not, no worries — I'll stop bothering you.

[Your Name]
```

**When to send:** 5 days after first email
**Follow-up:** If still no response, add to "nurture" list, try again in 30 days

---

### Template 3: Follow-Up #2 (Softer angle)
```
Subject: [Business Name] - just checking in

Hey [Owner Name],

Quick follow-up on the website thing. I know you might be busy, so no 
pressure at all.

But if you're ever curious what a $500 website looks like for your business, 
I'm happy to show you a mockup. Zero obligation.

Just hit reply or call me: [Phone]

[Your Name]
```

**When to send:** 10 days after first email (if no response to #2)

---

### Template 4: Referral Angle
```
Subject: Can I ask a favor?

[Owner Name],

I'm building websites for service businesses in [city], and I'd love to 
work with you OR someone you know.

If you know any [plumbers/contractors/salons] who could use an online 
presence, I'd appreciate the introduction.

If anyone books through your referral, I'll send them a discount and give 
you a $50 credit toward your site.

Thanks,
[Your Name]
```

**When to send:** For warm introductions/referrals
**Use this:** With customers you've already worked with

---

### Template 5: Urgency Email (If you're booked)
```
Subject: Last spot this month for [Business Name]

[Owner Name],

Got your email — thanks for responding!

Quick heads up: I'm almost booked for this month, so if you want your 
site built and live before [end of month], we need to get started this week.

If that works, let's jump on a call tomorrow. I have a 10am or 3pm slot.

[Your Name]
```

**When to send:** When you actually DO have limited availability
**Avoid:** Fake scarcity (kills trust if they find out)

---

### Template 6: Video Intro Email (Advanced)
```
Subject: 90-second video for [Business Name]

[Owner Name],

Instead of another email, I made a 90-second video showing what a website 
looks like for a business like yours.

[Link to Loom video or YouTube]

If it looks like something that could help you get more customers, let's 
chat.

[Your Name]
```

**When to send:** If you have video (creates 3x higher response rate)
**How to make:** Use Loom (free), show a template site customized for their business

---

### Template 7: The "I screwed up" Email (Breakup recovery)
```
Subject: My bad.

[Owner Name],

I've been hitting you with emails about a website, and I realize that's 
probably annoying.

Here's the truth: I'm trying to build my business, and you seem like 
someone who could actually benefit from going online. But I went about it 
the wrong way.

If you ever want to talk about it (no pressure), I'm here.

[Your Name]
```

**When to send:** After 3+ emails with zero response (humanizes you)
**Why it works:** Vulnerability = trust

## COLD CALL SCRIPTS

### Script 1: Basic Opener (Best for First Call)
```
[Dial]

"Hi, is this [Owner Name]? Great. This is [Your Name] calling. 
I work with [plumbers/contractors/salons] in [city] helping them 
get online. You got a quick minute?"

[If yes → continue]

"Cool. So I'm calling because I noticed you don't have a website yet. 
Quick question: are you losing customers to competitors who do have one?"

[Listen — they'll usually say yes or "maybe"]

"Yeah, that makes sense. Here's what I do: I build professional websites 
in 3 days for $500. Takes most people 2-3 hours of their time. 

Would it make sense to grab coffee this week and walk through what that 
looks like for your business? No commitment."

[If yes → schedule]
[If no → get email]
```

**Key timing:** Call early morning (8-9am) or late afternoon (4-5pm)
**Avoid:** Lunch (12-1pm), end of day (after 5pm)
**Best days:** Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday

---

### Script 2: If They Say "I'm busy"
```
"Totally get it. Quick question though — on a scale of 1-10, how much 
do you think you're losing by not being online?"

[Listen]

"Got it. Well, I can build your site in 3 days. So if you're open to 
it, maybe we grab 15 minutes this week? If it doesn't make sense, you're 
done with me."

[If still no → get email]
```

---

### Script 3: If They Say "Too expensive"
```
"What's your typical job worth? $500? $1000?"

[Listen]

"Exactly. So if this website gets you even ONE good customer, it pays 
for itself. And it usually does within the first month.

How about we just chat for 15 minutes and see if it makes sense?"
```

---

### Script 4: If They Say "I'll think about it"
```
"Sure, I get it. Quick question though: if I don't call you back, 
will you remember to look into this?"

[Usually they'll say no]

"Right. So how about this — I'll follow up with you Thursday at 2pm. 
If you're interested, we'll chat. If not, no hard feelings, yeah?"

[Confirm time]
```

**Why this works:** You're actually following up (instead of them forgetting), 
and you're removing the decision burden

---

### Script 5: If They Ask "Who did you build for?"
```
"I'm just starting out, so I don't have a ton of local examples yet. 
But I can show you some template sites and a mockup of what yours would 
look like. Fair?"

[If yes → schedule demo]

[Be honest here. If you've built 0 sites, don't lie about it. 
Instead, pivot: "Here's what I'm good at: I can build you a site 
in 3 days that'll get you found online. Want to see it?"]
```

## DOOR-TO-DOOR PITCH (In Person)

### Step 1: Walk In
- Go during business hours (not too busy)
- Dress clean (jeans + button-up is fine)
- Walk up to owner/manager, not front desk

---

### Step 2: The 30-Second Intro
```
"Hi, I'm [Name]. I'm helping local [businesses/plumbers/salons] in [city] 
get online. You got 30 seconds?"

[If yes, continue]

"Cool. So here's the deal: I build professional websites in 3 days for $500. 
Most businesses like yours are getting customers from Google, and you're 
probably losing some because you're not showing up.

I can fix that. Interested in a quick coffee chat about it?"

[If yes → take their number, offer to call next day]
[If no → hand them card: "In case you change your mind"]
```

---

### Step 3: Getting Their Info
Always ask:
```
"What's the best way to reach you? [Write down phone/email]

And what's the best time to call? Morning or afternoon?"
```

**Follow up:** Call them the next day (not texting first)

## LEAD TRACKING (Simple Airtable Setup)

### Fields You Need:
1. **Business Name**
2. **Owner Name**
3. **Phone**
4. **Email**
5. **Industry** (plumber, salon, etc.)
6. **City**
7. **Status** (prospect, contacted, meeting booked, closed, lost)
8. **Last Contact Date**
9. **Next Follow-up Date**
10. **Notes**
11. **Source** (cold email, cold call, referral, door-to-door)

### Statuses:
- **Prospect:** Haven't contacted yet
- **Contacted:** Reached out 1x, waiting to hear back
- **Contacted 2x:** Reached out 2x, still waiting
- **Meeting Booked:** They said yes to a call
- **Demo Scheduled:** Showed them mockup
- **Closed (Won):** They said yes, deposit received
- **Closed (Lost):** They said no or ghosted after 3+ contacts
- **Nurture:** Not interested now, might be later (follow up in 30 days)

### Daily Workflow:
1. **Morning:** Check "Next Follow-up Date" = today
2. **Make calls/send emails** to those contacts
3. **Update "Last Contact Date"** + **"Next Follow-up Date"**
4. **Check "Status"** — if "Meeting Booked", prep for call
5. **Evening:** Review day's wins/losses, update notes

## QUALIFICATION CHECKLIST (Before You Pitch)

Before spending time on someone, confirm:

- [ ] No existing website (or it's outdated/broken)
- [ ] They're actually the decision maker
- [ ] They seem open-minded (not dismissive)
- [ ] They have budget ($500 is affordable for them)
- [ ] They'd actually use the website (not just "having one"

If all ✓ → Schedule call. If any ✗ → Move on, don't waste time.

## RESPONSE RATES BY METHOD

| Method | Response Rate | Conversion Rate | Effort | Best For |
|--------|---------------|-----------------|--------|----------|
| Cold Email | 8-12% | 10-15% | Low | Scale (50+/week) |
| Cold Call | 25-35% | 15-25% | Medium | Efficiency |
| Door-to-Door | 60-80% | 30-40% | High | Certainty |
| LinkedIn | 5-8% | 5-10% | Low | Professional services |
| Referral | 70-90% | 50%+ | Varies | Leverage |

**Most efficient:** Mix door-to-door (build momentum) + cold calls (scale up)

## COMMON OBJECTIONS & RESPONSES

| Objection | Your Response |
|-----------|---------------|
| "Too expensive" | "What's one good customer worth to you? Probably more than $500, right?" |
| "I'm not interested" | "Fair. Can I ask why? Just want to understand." [Listen to their concern] |
| "I'll do it myself" | "You could. But do you want to? Most people don't. That's why I exist." |
| "I don't need a website" | "Maybe not. But your customers are searching for you online right now. Are they finding you?" |
| "Let me think about it" | "Sure. I'll check in Thursday. If you're interested we'll go from there." |
| "I want to talk to my [wife/partner]" | "Smart. When can all of us hop on a call? I can do [times]." |
| "Your price is too low" | "For now, yeah. As I get more customers, it'll go up. Good time to get in." |
| "Why should I trust you?" | "You shouldn't yet. That's why I'm offering a 3-day build. Try it. If you hate it, we're done." |

## CLOSING PHRASES THAT WORK

**To book a meeting:**
- "How about we grab coffee Thursday and I'll show you a mockup?"
- "I have a 10am or 2pm tomorrow. Which works?"
- "Let's just do a 15-minute call. No commitment."

**To close the deal:**
- "So we're doing this? I'll send you the contract and we'll get started Monday."
- "Just need 50% upfront to reserve your spot. I'll send an invoice."
- "When do you want this live? Let's work backwards from there."

**To handle hesitation:**
- "What's the worst that could happen? You get a website, it works, everyone's happy."
- "If you hate it, we can try something different. But we won't know until we build it."

## SUCCESS METRICS (WEEKLY)

Track these:
- **Outreach:** 50+ (calls + emails + visits)
- **Responses:** 5-7 (10-15%)
- **Meetings:** 2-3
- **Closes:** 0.5-1 (every 2 weeks minimum)

If you're not hitting these, adjust your:
- Messaging (template not resonating?)
- Targeting (wrong industry/city?)
- Method (email works better than calls for your vibe?)
