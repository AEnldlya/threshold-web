# Complete Sales Deployment Workflow
## From Cold Prospect to Live Website + Ongoing Revenue

---

## PHASE 1: FIND & RESEARCH PROSPECTS (2-3 hours for 20 prospects)

### Step 1.1: Identify Your Target List
**Method:** Google Maps + Local Directories (no API key needed)

**By business type:**
- Plumbers: Google "plumbers near [city]" → filter for 1-20 person shops
- Electricians: "electricians [city]" → independent/small shops
- Salons: "hair salons [city]" → single-location shops
- HVAC: "hvac contractors [city]"
- Restaurants: "restaurants [city]" → local independent spots
- Accountants: "accountants [city]" → solo/2-3 person firms
- Gyms: "gyms [city]" → independent fitness studios

**Qualification criteria:** They appear in Google Maps but:
- ❌ No website listed
- ❌ Website is outdated/broken (last updated 2010)
- ❌ Only have Facebook page
- ❌ Google My Business is bare (no description, 1-2 photos)

**Tools (free):**
- Google Maps
- Facebook
- Yelp
- Local business directories
- LinkedIn (for contact info)

### Step 1.2: Create Your Prospect Sheet
**Format: Simple spreadsheet (Google Sheets or Airtable)**

```
| Business Name | Owner/Contact | Phone | Email | City | Type | Current Web | Quality | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Bob's Plumbing | Bob Smith | 555-0123 | (found via FB) | Denver | Plumber | FB only | Good reviews | Truck graphics, team of 3 |
| Sally's Salon | Sally Jones | 555-0124 | sally@... | Denver | Salon | 2010 website | 50 reviews | Modern space, Instagram active |
| Mike's HVAC | Mike Davis | 555-0125 | mike@... | Denver | HVAC | None | No web | High Google reviews |
```

**Add columns:**
- Current web quality (none / broken / outdated / poor)
- Social media presence (what they're active on)
- Review count + rating (shows they have customers)
- Best contact method (phone / email / Facebook)
- Notes (what you'll reference in pitch)

### Step 1.3: Quick Web Research (2 min per prospect)
For each business:

1. **Google their name + city**
   - What comes up? Website? Only Google Maps? Facebook?
   - Are they ranking well without a website? (good sign = they'll see ROI)

2. **Check their Google My Business**
   - Click "Visit Website" — does it exist?
   - If yes, how old/bad is it?
   - Photos: 5+ or just 1-2?
   - Description: Full or blank?

3. **Facebook check**
   - Do they post regularly? (shows they market)
   - Good quality photos/updates? (shows they care about brand)
   - Website link in bio? (they want one)

4. **Phone/email**
   - Google Maps usually has phone
   - Email: try firstname@businessname.com or check Facebook page

**Red flags (skip these):**
- Already has modern website
- Chain/franchise (not independent decision-maker)
- Out of business indicators
- Only open seasonally

## PHASE 2: FIRST CONTACT - RESEARCH-BACKED PITCH (Phone or Email)

### Step 2.1: Cold Call Script (30 seconds, research-specific)

**DO NOT use generic opener. Reference what you found:**

```
Ring...

THEM: "Bob's Plumbing, this is Bob."

YOU: "Hi Bob, this is [Your Name]. I was looking at your Google Maps listing 
and saw you're getting great reviews — 4.8 stars with 47 reviews. 

Quick question: do you get a lot of calls from people searching 'plumber in Denver'?"

THEM: [Usually "yeah" or "some"]

YOU: "The reason I ask is, people searching that on Google right now... 
if they click on you, they go to [your website/Facebook]. 

I've been helping businesses in trades get a professional website 
that shows up in those searches — usually costs $500, takes 3 days to build.

Would it make sense to spend 10 minutes this week so I can show you what that looks like for you?"

THEM: [Yes/No/Maybe]

IF YES → Schedule 15-min demo call
IF NO → "No problem. Mind if I send you a quick example of what this looks like?"
IF MAYBE → "Perfect. How's Thursday afternoon?"
```

**Key tactics:**
- ✅ Reference their actual Google Maps/reviews (not generic)
- ✅ Ask permission before pitching ($500 is low-stakes)
- ✅ Offer demo, not commitment
- ✅ Get on calendar or permission to email

### Step 2.2: Email Version (If they don't answer phone)

**Subject: Quick idea for [Business Name] + Google**

```
Hi [Owner Name],

I was looking at your Google listing this week and noticed you've got 
[47 reviews, 4.8 stars] — that's solid.

One thing I've helped similar [trade] businesses with: 
most customers searching "plumber in Denver" never make it past the first page. 
Your Google listing is great, but a simple website helps you show up in more searches 
and gives customers a place to learn more about you.

I've been building simple 4-5 page sites for trades/services businesses 
for the last [X months]. Nothing fancy—just professional, mobile-friendly, 
shows your best work.

Builds take about 3 days. Cost is $500 all-in.

Would it be worth 10 minutes to see what that looks like for your business?

[Your Name]
[Phone]
```

**Send to:** Their email (if found) or Facebook message

## PHASE 3: DISCOVERY CALL / DEMO (15 minutes)

### Step 3.1: Research Before the Call
Before you hop on, have open:
- Their Google Maps listing
- Their current website (if it exists) / Facebook page
- Google Images of their work (if trades)

### Step 3.2: Discovery Questions (Ask, don't tell)

```
"Thanks for taking the time. A few quick questions:

1. How do most customers find you right now?"
   → (Phone? Google? Referrals? Social media?)

2. What's been your biggest challenge with getting customers?"
   → (Seasonal? Busy enough? Want to grow?)

3. Have you thought about a website before?"
   → (Why/why not? Budget concerns? Didn't think it mattered?)

4. If I showed you what your business could look like online, 
   what would make it worth doing?"
   → (More calls? Show credibility? Professionalism?)
```

**Listen more than you talk.**

### Step 3.3: Pitch (Show, Don't Tell)

"Here's what I'd build for you:"

**THEN SCREEN SHARE:**

1. **Their domain suggestion**
   ```
   "You'd own yoursite-denver.com 
   (or plumbingservice-denver.com if your name domain is taken)"
   ```

2. **Template mockup customized to THEIR business**
   - Show them a demo site in the same industry
   - OR show a Figma/screenshot mockup with their photos/text
   - Point out: phone prominently, hours, work samples, testimonials

3. **The ask**
   ```
   "Here's what I need from you:
   - 8-10 photos (storefront, team, work samples, before/afters)
   - Your service descriptions (I'll write it if you send me notes)
   - Hours, phone, email, address
   
   I'll have it live in 3 days. Then I set it up on your domain 
   so people can find you when they Google."
   ```

### Step 3.4: Close (Soft)

```
"Does this feel like something that would help you get more calls?"

[If YES]
"Great. Here's what happens: I'll send you a quick form 
with what I need from you (photos, business info). 
You send that back by [DATE]. I build it by [DATE]. 
We go live by [DATE].

Cost is $500. You can pay upfront or when it goes live. 
Sound good?"

[If NO]
"What's the hesitation?"
→ Handle objection (see below)

[If MAYBE]
"Fair enough. How about this: send me a couple photos 
and I'll build you a mockup to see if it's worth it?"
```

### Step 4.1: Send Asset Request Form

**Email / Google Form / Airtable**

```

Perfect! Here's what I need from you to build your site:

PHOTOS (8-10 total):
- Storefront/exterior (1-2)
- Team/staff (1-2) 
- Work samples or best service photos (3-4)

- Interior/workspace (optional)

BUSINESS COPY:
1. Your main services (bullet list is fine)
2. Why customers should choose you (2-3 sentences)
3. Any testimonials/reviews you love
4. Hours of operation
5. Service area / locations (if multi-area)

CONTACT INFO:
- Phone: ___
- Email: ___
- Address: ___
- Social media links: ___

DEADLINE: [DATE 2 days out] so I can deliver by [DATE]

Reply to this email or call me at [PHONE]

Thanks,
[Your Name]
```

### Step 4.2: Photo Triage + Optimization

When photos arrive:
1. **Organize them** by category
2. **Check quality** — lighting, focus, professionalism
3. **If photos are bad:**
   - Email: "Hey, got your photos. A couple are a bit dark. Can you reshoot [X] with better lighting?"
   - OR: "No problem. I know a photographer. Want me to schedule a 30-min shoot for $150?"
   - OR: Use stock photos (Unsplash/Pexels) as placeholder + note "replace with real photos"

**Pro tip:** Frame it as a gift, not a problem
```
"Your stuff looks great. I want to make sure the photos do it justice. 
A few reshoots would make a huge difference. Want me to handle it?"
```

### Step 4.3: Copy Editing

When they send copy:
- **Don't wait for perfect.** Use what they give you.
- **Fill gaps** with industry standards
- **Add testimonials** from their Google reviews (with attribution)

Example for plumber:
```
THEIR INPUT:

YOUR VERSION:

20+ years serving Denver metro. Licensed, insured, fast response times.
Same-day service available.
- Water heater repair/replacement
- Leak detection & repair

- New installation

Read our 47 five-star reviews on Google →"
```

## PHASE 6: DOMAIN SETUP & DEPLOYMENT

### Step 6.1: Domain Registration (15 min)

**Registrar options:**
- Namecheap ($12/year for .com, fast, easy)
- GoDaddy ($14/year, also common)
- Google Domains ($12/year, integrates with Google)

**Process:**
1. Search for availability:
   - `bobsplumbing.com`
   - `bobsplumbing-denver.com`
   - `denverplumbing.pro`
   - Pick best available

2. Purchase for 2 years (show client you're committed)
   - **You pay:** $24 for 2 years
   - **Client gets:** Domain + hosting for year 1 (included in $500 deal)
   - **After year 1:** They renew $12/year, or pay you $50/year to manage

3. **Set up DNS to point to Netlify** (see Step 6.2)

### Step 6.2: Deploy to Netlify (15 min)

**Option A: Drag-and-drop (simplest)**
1. Create folder: `bob-plumbing-site/`
2. Add: index.html, css/, js/, images/
3. Go to netlify.com → "Add new site" → drag folder
4. Netlify auto-deploys → get temporary URL
5. Update domain DNS:
   - Go to Namecheap
   - Add DNS records:
     ```
     Type: A
     Host: @
     Value: [Netlify's IP, usually 75.2.60.5]
     
     Type: CNAME
     Host: www
     Value: [your-site-name].netlify.app
     ```
6. Update Netlify domain setting:
   - Domain Management → Add custom domain → `bobsplumbing-denver.com`
   - Netlify auto-configures

**Option B: GitHub + Netlify (if you want version control)**
1. Create GitHub repo: `bob-plumbing-site`
2. Push files to GitHub
3. Connect GitHub repo to Netlify (auto-deploys on push)
4. Same domain setup as above

**Option C: Just use Netlify's auto-generated URL**
- `bob-plumbing-site.netlify.app`
- No custom domain (cheaper for you, less professional for them)

**Recommendation: Do custom domain** — costs you $24 (domain) + $0 (Netlify free tier) = $24 total cost, they get professional presence.

### Step 6.3: HTTPS + Performance (5 min)

- **Netlify auto-provides HTTPS** — no extra work needed
- **Speed check:** 
  - Go to PageSpeed Insights (pagespeed.web.dev)
  - Test your site
  - If images too large, optimize:
    ```bash
    # Locally, reduce image sizes
    convert image.jpg -resize 1200x800 -quality 85 image-optimized.jpg
    ```
  - Re-deploy

### Step 6.4: Email Setup (Optional, 15 min)

If they want professional email:

**Option 1: Namecheap email** (~$0.88/month)
- Register with Namecheap → add email
- bob@bobsplumbing-denver.com
- Forward to their personal email

**Option 2: Google Workspace** ($6/month)
