# Phone Pitch Script for Website Sales

## Before You Call
1. **Do a 10-second website check**: Google "[Business Name] website" - if they have one, skip them
2. **Best times to call**: 2-4 PM (avoid lunch, breakfast rush for restaurants; avoid 8-9 AM for service businesses)
3. **Speak to manager/owner**: Ask "Is the owner or manager available?" - don't pitch to staff
4. **Keep it short**: 60 seconds max. You're not selling on the phone - you're getting a meeting

---

## OPENING (10 seconds)
```
"Hi [Name], this is Andy calling from [Company]. Quick question - 
are you the owner or manager? I noticed you don't have an online 
presence and I wanted to reach out because we're working with 
[similar business] in Boston and they're getting great results."
```

**Why this works:**
- Establishes you're not spam (name + company)
- Gets decision maker on phone
- References similar business (social proof)
- Mentions they DON'T have website (they know it's true - no defensiveness)

---

## VALUE PROPOSITION (20 seconds)
Pick ONE based on business type:

### For Restaurants:
```
"Most customers search online before they eat anywhere. If you 
show up on Google Maps and have a simple site with your menu, 
hours, and location - you'll get more walk-in traffic. We just 
built one for [another restaurant]. Takes 3 days, costs $500."
```

### For Service Businesses (Plumbing, HVAC, Electric):
```
"When someone needs an emergency plumber at 10 PM, they Google 
'plumber near me.' If you're not there - they call someone else. 
A simple site with your photo, phone, and reviews gets you 50% 
more calls. $500, 3 days."
```

### For Salons/Beauty:
```
"Younger customers expect to see you online - hours, photos of 
your work, booking. A professional site builds trust and gets 
appointment requests 24/7. $500, we handle everything."
```

---

## HANDLE OBJECTIONS (10 seconds each)

**"We don't need a website"**
```
"Fair enough. Most said the same thing 6 months ago. Want to 
see a quick example of what one looks like? 2-minute call?"
```

**"We have Facebook"**
```
"Facebook is great for content. But when someone searches for 
your business, they don't find Facebook - they find Google. 
Let me show you the difference."
```

**"How much does it cost?"**
```
"$500 one-time. No monthly fees. You own it. We build, you own 
the domain. Want to see what it includes?"
```

**"We're too busy"**
```
"Totally get it. That's why we handle everything - you just 
provide a couple photos and your hours. We build the rest."
```

---

## ASK FOR THE MEETING (20 seconds)

**If interested:**
```
"Cool. Let me send you an example of a site we did for a 
[similar business]. Takes 2 minutes to look at. If you like 
it, we can set up a quick call. Sound good?"
```

**Get their email:**
```
"What's the best email to send that to?"
```

**Then:**
- Email them a LIVE EXAMPLE SITE (not just screenshots)
- Follow up 24 hours later
- Close the deal on second call

---

## CLOSE (If they're ready to say yes):

```
"Perfect. Here's how we work: You give us 3-4 photos and your 
hours/phone. We build the site in 3 days on a temporary link so 
you can review. Once you approve - we move it to your domain for 
$500. Fair?"
```

---

## KEY TACTICS

✅ **Use their name** - shows respect
✅ **Mention competitors** - creates urgency ("they're already doing this")
✅ **Keep it to 60 seconds** - respect their time
✅ **Ask permission before pitching** - "Quick question?" vs. launching into sales
✅ **Get them to say YES to a small ask first** - "Can I send an example?" is easier than "$500 website"
✅ **Email the example SAME DAY** - momentum matters
✅ **Follow up 24 hours later** - most sales happen on 2nd contact
✅ **Be honest** - "3 days, $500, you own it" is your entire pitch

## WHAT TO SEND IN EMAIL

Subject: "[Business Name] - Quick Example"

Body:
```
Hi [Name],

Following up from our call. Here's a real example of a site 
we built for [similar business]:

[LIVE LINK - not screenshot]

Takes 3 minutes to look at. The owner went from 0 online 
presence to getting bookings/inquiries through the site.

Yours would be similar - your photos, menu/services, hours, 
phone, map location. $500 one-time, you own it.

Interested in a 10-minute call to walk through yours?

-Andy
P.S. This is what you'd own (live on your domain, forever)
```

---

## NUMBERS YOU NEED TO TRACK

Use a simple spreadsheet:
- Called? (Y/N)
- Interested? (Y/N)
- Sent example? (Y/N)
- Followed up? (Y/N)
- Closed? (Y/N)
- Date of close
- $ received

**Target**: 20 calls/day = ~2-3 interested = ~1-2 deals/week = $500-1000/week = $2K-4K/month

Good luck. Call one, see how it feels, adjust the pitch.
