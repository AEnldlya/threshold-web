# Scaling Playbook: 1 Site/Month → 5-10 Sites/Month

Once you close your first site, the bottleneck shifts.

At 1 site/month: You're limited by **outreach** (not enough leads)
At 5 sites/month: You're limited by **time** (can't build fast enough)
At 10 sites/month: You're limited by **quality** (rushing kills reputation)

This playbook shows you how to scale through each phase.

---

## PHASE 1: 1-2 SITES/MONTH (Months 1-2)

**Your constraint:** Getting leads

**Your job:**
- Outreach (50-100 contacts/week)
- Closing (1-2 deals/month)
- Building (follow checklist exactly)
- Learning (improve process each site)

**What to measure:**
- Contacts/week
- Response rate
- Meetings booked
- Close rate
- Time per build (aiming to get under 12 hours)

**What to NOT do:**
- Don't hire anyone yet
- Don't invest in fancy tools
- Don't perfect the process (good enough wins)
- Don't build custom designs (use template base)

**Revenue:** $500-1000/month (from builds) + $40-80/month (maintenance)

---

## PHASE 2: 3-4 SITES/MONTH (Months 3-4)

**Your constraint:** Still getting enough leads, but builds are getting tighter

**You've figured out:**
- What outreach works (cold calls? door-to-door? email?)
- Your close rate (you know how many calls = 1 deal)
- Your build process (12 hours per site is standard)

**Now optimize:**

### Step 1: Double Down on What Works
If door-to-door converts at 40% and cold email at 10%:
- Stop emailing entirely
- Do 20 door-to-door visits/week instead
- Expected: 8 conversations = 1-2 meetings = 0.5 closes (hitting 2/month)

If cold calls work:
- Make 20 calls/day (3 hours)
- 30% response = 6 conversations
- 50% of conversations = 3 meetings
- 50% of meetings = 1-2 closes

**Result:** 3-4 sites/month is now possible with focused outreach

### Step 2: Optimize Your Build Process
Current: 12 hours per site

**Ways to get to 8-10 hours:**
- Create a template base for each industry (plumber, salon, etc.)
- Pre-build common sections (hero, services, contact, footer)
- Use copy templates (you've written these 3 times, now paste + customize)
- Batch similar tasks (build all homepages first, then all services pages)
- Set up a "design system" (same colors, fonts, spacing everywhere)

**New time:** 8-10 hours per site

### Step 3: Streamline Client Feedback
Current: 3 rounds of revisions

**Optimize:**
- Strict timeline (feedback by 5pm or we move without it)
- 2 rounds max (Day 1 mockup gets 1 round, Day 2 pages get 1 round)
- "No scope creep" clause (changes after launch are a separate fee)

**Result:** Saves 1-2 hours per site

### Step 4: Template Everything
Create templates for:
- **Kickoff email** (copy-paste with client name)
- **Feedback request email** (same format every time)
- **Launch notification email** (template)
- **Contract** (fill in blanks)
- **Invoice** (auto-generate)

**Result:** 1-2 hours saved per site on admin

**New total:** 6-8 hours per site (down from 12)

### What This Means
- 3-4 sites/month × 7 hours each = 21-28 hours work
- That's 5-7 hours/week of building
- Rest is outreach + admin
- You can do this solo

---

## PHASE 3: 5-8 SITES/MONTH (Months 5-6)

**Your constraint:** Time. You can't personally build more than ~30 hours/week

**Decision point:** Do you want to scale, or stay at 3-4/month?

### Option A: Stay at 3-4/month (Smart Choice)
- Keep your time at 30 hours/week
- 20 hours building, 10 hours outreach/admin
- $2K/month revenue (builds) + $120-160/month (maintenance)
- High quality, happy customers, sustainable
- This is a good business

### Option B: Scale to 5-8/month (Growth Choice)
This requires delegation.

**Your options:**

#### Option B1: Hire a Virtual Assistant (Cheapest)
Cost: $300-500/month

**What they do:**
- Admin (invoicing, contracts, scheduling calls)
- Content gathering (emails, file management)
- Quality checks (load testing, broken links)
- Lead follow-up (email reminders, scheduling)

**What you do:**
- Outreach (sales calls, door-to-door)
- Building (design + code)
- Client calls (kickoffs, demos)

**Time saved:** 5-7 hours/week (admin + follow-up)

**New capacity:** Can now build 5-8 sites/month (5-6 hours per build with less admin overhead)

**Revenue:** $2500-4000/month (builds) + $200-320/month (maintenance) minus VA cost (-$400)

#### Option B2: Hire a Junior Designer/Developer (Better)
Cost: $2000-3000/month (contract) or $30-40K/year (FTE)

**What they do:**
- Builds sites from your designs
- Front-end code (HTML/CSS)
- Content input
- Testing

**What you do:**
- Outreach (sales)
- Design direction + mockups
- Client meetings
- QA + final touches

**Time saved:** 50% on building
**New capacity:** 8-12 sites/month (you do mockups in 1-2 hours, they build in 4-5)

**Revenue:** $4000-6000/month (builds) minus contractor cost (-$2500) = **$1500-3500/month profit**

**This is where it gets good.**

#### Option B3: Build a Team (Best Long-Term)
Cost: $50K-100K/year (2-3 team members)

**Team:**
- You: Sales + Design direction
- Designer: Mockups + design
- Developer: Builds sites
- Admin: Handles all logistics

**Capacity:** 15-25 sites/month

**Revenue:** $7500-12500/month (builds) + $600/month (maintenance) minus team costs (-$8000) = **$200-4500/month profit**

**Note:** At this scale, you shift from "doing the work" to "managing people who do the work." Different skill set. Many people find it less fun.

## MY RECOMMENDATION

**Months 1-2:** 1-2 sites/month (learn + build, perfectionism okay)
**Months 3-4:** 3-4 sites/month (optimize, templates, streamline)
**Months 5+:** Stay at 3-4/month + build maintenance base

This gives you:
- **Year 1:** $6000 revenue (builds) + $480 (maintenance) = $6480
- **Year 2:** $18000 revenue (36 builds at $500) + $4800 (40 customers at $40/month) = **$22800**

OR, if you want to scale:

**Months 5-6:** Hire VA + junior dev (add ~$3000/month cost)
**Months 7-12:** Ramp to 8-12 sites/month
- **Year 1 (after scaling):** $24000 (builds) + $1920 (maintenance) minus $18000 (salaries) = **$7920**
- **Year 2:** $48000 (builds) + $4800 (maintenance) minus $36000 (salaries) = **$16800**

**By Year 3:** Potentially $100K+ revenue, $50K+ profit if you're good at it.

## WHAT TO FOCUS ON AT EACH PHASE

### Phase 1 (1-2 sites):
- [ ] Get really good at sales (this is your bottleneck)
- [ ] Nail your build process (test it 2-3 times)
- [ ] Track everything (Airtable discipline)
- [ ] Get testimonials + case studies (after each win)
- [ ] Measure: Outreach volume, close rate, time per build

### Phase 2 (3-4 sites):
- [ ] Pick one outreach method that works (stop trying others)
- [ ] Create industry templates (plumber/salon/restaurant)
- [ ] Set strict timelines (feedback by X time or we proceed)
- [ ] Build a copy library (reusable content blocks)
- [ ] Measure: Close rate (should be 30%+), time per build (should be <10 hours)

### Phase 3 (5-8 sites):
- [ ] Hire help (VA for admin, or junior for building)
- [ ] Document your process (so others can follow it)
- [ ] Build systems (so you're not the bottleneck)
- [ ] Focus on sales (bring in the deals, let team build them)
- [ ] Measure: Revenue per hour, profit after costs, customer satisfaction

## THE REFERRAL FLYWHEEL

Once you hit 10-15 happy customers, referrals become your #1 lead source.

**Why?**
- Happy customer tells 1-2 friends
- Friend buys
- Now you have 2 happy customers
- They tell 2 friends each
- Now you have 4 happy customers

**Month 1:** 1 customer (from cold outreach)
**Month 2:** 2 customers (1 cold, 1 referral)
**Month 3:** 3-4 customers (1 cold, 2-3 referrals)
**Month 4:** 5-8 customers (1-2 cold, 4-6 referrals)

By month 4, **referrals are doing your selling for you.**

**How to make referrals happen:**
1. Do great work (non-negotiable)
2. Ask directly: "If you know someone who could use this, I'd love an intro"
3. Give referral credit: "Give them my number, and I'll send you $50 credit"
4. Stay in touch: $40/month maintenance means you email them every month anyway
5. Track referrals: Who referred who? Send them a thank-you note

## WHEN TO HIRE (Specific Triggers)

**Hire a VA when:**
- [ ] You're spending >5 hours/week on admin
- [ ] You're turning down projects because of admin
- [ ] You have a waiting list (people want to buy but you're full)

**Hire a developer when:**
- [ ] You're spending >20 hours/week building
- [ ] Clients are waiting >1 week for their sites
- [ ] You have a waiting list
- [ ] You can afford $2000-3000/month

## SYSTEMS THAT SCALE

These are the things that let you grow without losing quality:

1. **Template base** (v4/v5 sites customized for industry)
2. **Content library** (copy blocks you reuse)
3. **Process checklist** (exactly what to do each day)
4. **Feedback system** (strict timeline, 2 rounds max)
5. **Lead database** (Airtable, tracking everything)
6. **Contract/invoice templates** (copy-paste + fill blanks)
7. **Quality checklist** (testing process before launch)
8. **Client onboarding** (same call, same questions, same handoff)

These 8 systems let you go from 1 → 10 sites/month without everything falling apart.

## THE TRUTH ABOUT SCALING

**Small is easier.** 3-4 sites/month solo is way easier than 8 sites with a team.

**Big is more profitable.** But only if you're good at it. Bad operators at scale are just bad + bigger.

**You don't have to scale.** A solo operation at $2K/month profit is a fine business. It's a job that pays better than most jobs.

**You should scale if:** (a) You want more money, (b) You like building teams, (c) You want to delegate the building to focus on sales

**You should stay small if:** (a) You like hands-on work, (b) You value free time over money, (c) Quality matters more than quantity

Both are valid. Pick what YOU want, not what you think you should want.
